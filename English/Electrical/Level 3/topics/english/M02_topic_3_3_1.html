2.3.1 Voltage Drop Example

To determine the voltage drop on an existing 120V, two-wire installation or a proposed project, find the voltage drop of the circuit by using one of the single-phase formulas:

V D = ( 2 × L × K × I ) ÷ C M

For example, assume that a 120V, two-wire circuit feeds a total load of 30A with a branch circuit length of 120'.

NEC Table 310.15(B)(16) allows a No. 10 AWG conductor at 60°C (140°F) to carry this load. (This does not consider voltage drop or derating factors.) NEC Chapter 9, Table 8 shows that the area (A) of a No. 10 AWG conductor is 10,380 CM. Substitute these values and a value of 12.9 (copper) for K in the equation:

V D = 2 × 120 ' × 12.9 × 30 A ÷ 10,380   C M
V D = 8.95 V

The voltage at the load is 120V − 8.95V = 111.05V, which is usually not acceptable on most installations because it exceeds the 3% recommended maximum voltage drop (3% of 120 = 3.6V). In fact, it is more than double. There are three ways to correct this situation:

  • Reduce the load

  • Increase the conductor size

  • Increase the voltage
