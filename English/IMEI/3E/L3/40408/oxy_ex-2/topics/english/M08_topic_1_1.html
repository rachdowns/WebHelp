Overview

Data networks make it possible for computers and other intelligent devices to share information. In an industrial setting, data networks are used to control and monitor plant operations. They receive temperature, pressure, flow rate, and level information from sensors, allowing operators and maintenance personnel to monitor the status of all plant operations. Networks also allow operators to change operating parameters and control plant operations from a central location. Electrical and instrumentation technicians must be familiar with data networks because data networks play such an important role in monitoring and controlling manufacturing and industrial process equipment.

Objectives

When you have completed this module, you will be able to do the following:

  1. Describe how data networks are used in industrial facilities.

  2. Identify the types of data networks used in industrial facilities and describe the methods used to control information flow within a network.

  3. Describe how open connectivity is used in industrial data networks.

  4. Identify the types of cables used to connect computers and other devices within a network and explain their applications.

  5. Describe the physical layer of two or more device buses.

  6. Apply connectors to UTP and coaxial cable.

Performance Tasks

Under the supervision of the instructor, the trainee should be able to do the following:

  1. Properly run and terminate CAT 6 and coaxial cables.
