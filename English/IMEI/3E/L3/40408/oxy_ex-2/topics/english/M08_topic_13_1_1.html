12.1.1 Repeaters

Repeaters are used primarily in IEEE 802.3 (or Ethernet) networks. Repeaters are used to make IEEE 802.3 media transitions or to extend IEEE 802.3 networks when distance limitations are reached. A repeater will retime and regenerate the digital signals and send them on their way again. More commonly today, a fiber link is installed to extend the connections.
