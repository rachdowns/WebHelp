Glossary

Arc flash

: Dangerous condition caused by the release of energy in an electric arc, usually associated with electrical distribution equipment.

Backfeed

: To energize a section of a power network from a source of supply other than its normal source.

Electrically safe work condition

: A state in which the conductor or circuit part to be worked on or near has been disconnected from energized parts, locked/tagged in accordance with established standards, tested to ensure the absence of voltage, and grounded if determined necessary.

Equipotential

: Describes a service that has the same electrical potential at all points.

Ground

: Conducting connection, whether intentional or accidental, between an electrical circuit or equipment and the earth, or to some conducting body that serves in place of the earth.

Ground fault

: Leakage of electrical current to ground.

Hot stick

: Sometimes called shotgun stick or clamp stick. An insulated device primarily used for voltage testing and the attachment or removal of temporary grounding devices to station busses. This device must be used and tested in accordance with the requirements associated with live-line tools in OSHA 29, CFR1910.269.

Personal protective ground (PPG)

: Bonding jumper that is intentionally installed to ground de-energized, normally ungrounded circuit conductors when personnel are working on them, to minimize voltage differences between different parts of the equipment and personnel, so as to protect against shock hazards and/or equipment damage.

Qualified person

: One who has skills and knowledge related to the construction and operation of the electrical equipment and installations and has received safety training on the hazards involved.

Temporary protective grounds (TPGs)

: A secondary safety measure used to ensure personnel safety by electrically connecting de-energized current-carrying conductors to each other to ensure that all are at ground potential. These devices also protect personnel from inadvertently induced voltages, and are installed and removed only by qualified persons.
