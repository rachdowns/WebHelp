9.1.0 Installation of Temporary Grounding Devices

The first action of installing temporary grounding devices is to make sure that all connection points are clean. After that is completed, connect one end of the down lead cable to the metal structure or ground bus of the switchgear (pole, or tower) before connecting the other end to a phase conductor of the de-energized equipment. Then, and only then, should the grounding cables be connected between phase conductors. Phase-to-ground grounding (parallel grounding) consists of a TPG from each phase conductor to a common ground riser, or approved ground attachment point (Figure 19).

Since working on live voltage is dangerous, the activities presented in this module are to be performed only on de-energized and locked out electrical equipment set up for training purposes. After you have proven that you can install and remove temporary grounding devices, you may be allowed to train on live-voltage equipment.

There are hundreds of different situations where temporary grounding devices could be used, but for training purposes, only the installation and removal of two simple grounding cable assemblies (cables with clamps) will be performed here.

The task is to attach the ground electrode clamp of each temporary grounding assembly to a fixed grounding electrode, and then use a hot stick (with the appropriate attachment) to install the line-end clamp to a de-energized overhead power line. Your instructor will have specification information about the power line, such as the amount of voltage and current normally carried by the line and any possible stored energy in the area, but you must ask for that information. Knowing what to ask for is part of your training. After your trainer approves the installation, you will remove and store the grounding assemblies. Perform the following steps to install temporary PPGs.

  1. Determine the level of voltage and current carried by the power distribution line(s).

  2. Determine if there are any stored energy points to be concerned with.

  3. Determine the attachment points on the line(s).

  4. Determine and collect the type of grounding devices needed for the job:

    • Ampacity (current rating) required of the grounding devices

    • Type of clamps

    • Length of cables

    • Type and length of hot stick

    • Type of hot stick attachment(s)

  5. Inspect (and clean as necessary) all the PPG equipment to be used.

  6. Check the PPG items to verify that they are rated to handle any potential fault current that may be encountered.

  7. Obtain any permits or clearances required to work near any electrical lines.

    Note:

    NOTE

    Some companies require a permit or clearance authorization to be signed by a manager before any high-voltage work is performed.

  8. Coordinate the interruption of power to the distribution line.

  9. Locate and disconnect power to the line.

  10. Install appropriate lock(s) and tag(s).

  11. Transport all grounding devices and associated tools to the work site.

  12. Install any area barricades as required.

  13. Locate permanent grounding electrode(s) and equipment connection points. Clean as necessary.

  14. Select the hot stick attachment needed for the job and install it onto the hot stick.

    Warning:

    WARNING!

    Class 0 (for up to 750 volts) or Class 1 (for 750 volts or higher) voltage-rated gloves are required when using hot sticks for voltage testing or for installing/removing temporary grounds. Class 3 gloves are not required for applications when using a hot stick because the hot stick provides the primary protection.

  15. Attach the ground electrode clamp to the first permanent grounding electrode.

  16. Determine where to install the line clamp end of the first grounding cable to the overhead power line.

    Note:

    NOTE

    The first PPG should be installed upstream nearest the supply end of the power line. The second PPG will be installed downstream of the machine or work site.

  17. Ensure that the installation (contact) area on the overhead power line is clean. Clean if necessary.

  18. Open the jaws of the line clamp enough for the clamp to be installed onto the overhead line.

  19. Use the hot stick’s attachment to pick up and lift the line clamp end of the grounding cable toward the contact area on the overhead line.

    Warning:

    WARNING!

    To prevent accidental contact with another line or other electrical equipment, always be very careful when moving a PPG into the installation position.

  20. Move the hot stick as needed to install the clamp onto the overhead line.

  21. Use the hot stick’s attachment to tighten the clamp’s screw adjustment to secure the clamp to the line.

  22. Remove the hot stick from the installed clamp.

  23. Determine where to install the line clamp end of the second PPG to the overhead power line.

  24. Ensure that the second contact area of the overhead line is clean and clear.

  25. Connect the second PPG to the nearest grounding electrode, and then connect its line clamp to the overhead line.

  26. Secure the second line clamp and then remove the hot stick.

  27. Ask your instructor to evaluate your work.

    Note:

    NOTE

    Install temporary grounding cables phase-to-phase on different lines if the resources are available and the instructor permits it.
