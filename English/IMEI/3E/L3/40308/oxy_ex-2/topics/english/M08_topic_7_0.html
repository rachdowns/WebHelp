Section Six

6.0.0 Temporary Grounding Preparations

You have learned why and where temporary grounding devices are used, and some of the terms associated with such work. This section explains some procedures involved in using temporary grounding devices.

According to NFPA 70B, Sections 7.4.2, 7.4.3, and 7.4.4, always start by placing the workplace (equipment, power lines, etc.) into an electrically safe work condition. Ensure that all power sources are properly disconnected, locked out, and tagged. To help you verify that the workplace is in a safe work condition, follow these procedures:

  • Check applicable, up-to-date drawing diagrams and identification tags to determine all possible sources of supply to the specific equipment. Determine the maximum voltage level of each electrical source. Ensure that the proper disconnecting device for each source is identified.

  • Ensure that a job/task safety review is completed and all team members are briefed prior to starting the work. Coordinate the shutdown of the identified equipment or circuits. Make sure to obtain and wear only PPE rated for the job.

  • Interrupt the load current from each circuit and then open the source disconnecting device(s) to the circuit. Verify that each disconnecting device for each source is properly opened. In cases where visible blade disconnecting devices are used, visually verify that all blades are fully open. If draw-out circuit breakers are used, ensure that they are withdrawn to the fully disconnected position.

  • Apply lockout/tagout devices in accordance with documented and established policies. If fuses are removed to de-energize the equipment, secure them to prevent their unauthorized reinsertion.

  • Obtain an adequately rated voltage detector that can be used to confirm that all circuit conductors are de-energized (this is the live-dead-live test). Before and after each test, use the voltage detector on a known circuit to verify that it is operating satisfactorily. Use the voltage detector to verify that each phase conductor or circuit part is de-energized. Test each phase conductor or circuit part both phase-to-phase and phase-to-ground. These tests are especially important on circuits involving switches and fixed-type circuit breakers in which the blades cannot be visually checked.

  • Never touch untested and ungrounded conductors. Consider all conductors energized until they are tested and confirmed to be de-energized. After verifying that the affected conductors have no voltage on them, ground them adequately in accordance with established procedures for installing temporary grounds. Grounding the de-energized conductors protects personnel in the event that, in spite of all precautions, the equipment does become re-energized. When capacitors are involved, short them to ground to drain off any stored charges. After capacitors have been discharged, install a shorting strap and temporary ground to keep them from being accidently re-energized.

  • Work on only one circuit at a time.

  • Ensure that all personnel associated with the work are involved in verifying that the affected circuits and conductors have no voltage. Each individual should personally verify that all necessary steps were executed in the proper manner.

  • When it is necessary to work near energized equipment, install barriers (ropes around the work area; insulated rubber blankets for isolation), and use insulated tools and rubber gloves approved for the voltage levels expected.
