2.3.0 MOV Operation Control

Most failures of MOVs are due to incorrect sizing and selection of the actuator motor, damage to the valve components due to corrosion and wear, and motor overloads resulting from over-travel of the valve seat when opening or closing.

Valves have physical or mechanical limits. Fully opened or fully closed are their two extreme limits, but valves are also opened or closed to specific locations between their extreme limits. Mechanical devices attached to the valve stems indicate where the valve is positioned (opened, closed, or somewhere in between). Electronic sensing devices monitor the position of these mechanical devices and transmit that information back to the system controlling the MOV. The sensing devices are either a limit switch, torque switch, or limiting valve. They are integral parts of MOVs because they signal the unit to start and stop. These devices prevent the destruction of the equipment that would otherwise occur. Limit switches and torque switches are often mechanical devices that measure the travel of the valve stem or the amount of torque created by the driving device and actuator. The principle is that the valve must move far enough to ensure a tight seal, but not far enough to damage the seals, valve seat, gears, and other components. The MOV must stop before the equipment (drive motor, actuator, or valve) is damaged. Figure 4 shows the electrical component compartment of a Limitorque L120-85 actuator.

The Limitorque L120 is one of the more typical valve actuators found on larger valves. Others may be larger or smaller, but most require similar attention, which includes cleaning and lubrication. The timing and positioning of the cams and switches used in these actuators is extremely delicate. Any drag on their movement, or extreme wear on them, can disrupt their timing.

Limit switches and torque switches must be lubricated according to the manufacturer’s recommendations. Maintenance technicians must follow manufacturer’s recommendations for compatible lubricants.

Valve Conversions

Manually operated valves can be converted to MOVs. Some manufacturers perform this conversion service at their facility. Valve manufacturers also have kits available that will allow field personnel to convert the valves.
