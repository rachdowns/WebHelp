Section Four

4.0.0 Setup Activities for MOVs

Motor-operated valves must be set up and operated correctly because in some cases, an improperly operating valve can result in a catastrophic system failure. Federal and state guidelines often require companies to create a manual of written procedures for maintaining MOVs. Technicians must follow those procedures, to the letter in some cases, when they work on a MOV. These manuals must be updated at required intervals. Manuals applicable to specific performance tasks must be kept on site and at the locations where the work is performed. These manuals are intended to ensure that all efforts are made to keep the operational system functioning as designed. A valve maintenance log is used to record information about work done on the MOVs (Figure 17).

On highly critical MOVs, the personnel working on those MOVs must be properly trained and certified. Their training and certification must be documented. When regulatory inspectors arrive to inspect critical operations, such as pipeline operations or nuclear plants, they normally look at the training and certification records of personnel working on specific equipment items, such as the MOVs.

Installation procedures will vary depending on the type of valve, type of valve actuator, manufacturer, and the location of the installation. The following sections provide some broad examples of how MOVs might be set up for operation. When installing or maintaining MOVs or any other equipment, always follow company policies and procedures and the manufacturer’s recommendations and instructions for the exact units.
