Summary

A motor-operated valve (MOV) consists of some type of motor, an actuator, and a valve. The purpose of the actuator is to convert power from the motor into valve movement to open, close, or set a valve. In order to maintain safe and efficient operation, MOVs must be monitored, well maintained, and regularly adjusted.

To perform mechanical work on MOVs, you must be trained and qualified and have a strong mechanical aptitude, as well as a certain amount of problem-solving ability. Troubleshooting an MOV can be a long, tedious process requiring much patience. Like any other piece of equipment, an MOV can be complex. The ability to stick to the task enables you to make a well thought-out diagnosis. You can then complete a repair or adjustment efficiently and return the system to normal. Discipline is required to follow the many complex procedures required by manufacturer’s instructions and company policies and procedures.

Maintaining safe and efficient operation of MOVs is critical to the proper operation of the system that these valves are controlling. In order to do this, MOVs must be maintained in good operating condition. It is up to you to learn, study, and train to become qualified to maintain such important equipment.
