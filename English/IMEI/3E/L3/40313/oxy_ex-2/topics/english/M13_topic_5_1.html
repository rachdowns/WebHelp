4.1.0 Initial Installation

When the decision is made to install a MOV onto a valve, the following factors must be taken into consideration:

  • Where is the valve located?

  • What kind of environment will it be operating in (inside a clean processing area, in a corrosive environment [inside or outside], or maybe outdoors in the weather)?

  • How fast must it function?

  • How is it to be powered and controlled?

  • Does its stem rotate or slide in and out?

  • How much torque will be needed to open and close the valve?

A coupling is needed to connect the MOV drive to the valve stem. If the coupling is not supplied by the MOV manufacturer, it will have to be fabricated on site. If the valve stem is threaded, the MOV coupling may have to be machined to match the valve stem threads.

If the MOV is driven by a motor, the rotation of the drive motor must be verified before the MOV is mounted onto the valve. If the motor is to rotate clockwise to close and counterclockwise to open, those movements must be verified before the motor is installed. The same applies if cylinders are being used. The directional controls must be able to control the MOV before it is installed onto the valve.

When the MOV is ready to be mounted, it must be aligned to the valve’s open or closed position. Because a MOV needs to know where the limits are on the valve, adjustable travel stops are mounted on the valve body or the mounting bracket between the MOV and valve. Some valves have travel indicators and travel indicator scales mounted on them so that anyone near the valve can tell how far the valve is opened or closed. When adapting a MOV to its valve, the travel stops must be properly positioned or the MOV will not function properly. Most quarter-turn valves are equipped with adjustable travel stops to limit valve travel. These travel stops are usually threaded studs with locking nuts, setscrews, or other devices to prevent the setting from changing.

After the MOV has been properly orientated and securely attached to the valve body, it should be tested to verify that it will open or close the valve. Most MOVs can be jogged or moved a little at a time. If the MOV controls open or close the valve properly, then the fine-tuning part of the setup can begin.
