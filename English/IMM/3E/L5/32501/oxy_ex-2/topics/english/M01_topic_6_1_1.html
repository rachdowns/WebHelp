5.1.1 Heating Devices

In most cases, the heating devices used in a refinery will be designed specifically for the applications in which they are being used. They will usually be vertical and boxlike in design. Such heating devices may be electrically powered, or they may be capable of burning almost anything. Most furnaces and heaters found on a refinery site will be burning fossil fuels.

Heaters and furnaces are useful only if they deliver heat to some process. The heat from them must be transferred to another vessel. That other vessel is usually a boiler or heat exchanger filled with liquid. There are two types of boilers: fire-tube boilers and water-tube boilers.

The hot exhaust air from a typical furnace is drawn through piping and a filtering system by large fans located near the chimney or stack. In a fire-tube boiler, that hot exhaust air is passed through tubes located inside a vessel containing water. The heat of the hot air inside the tubes is transferred through the walls of the tubes and into the water surrounding them. After the heated exhaust air passes through the submerged tubes, the filtering system cleans it before it is released into the atmosphere. Figure 12 shows the open end of a fire-tube boiler.

In a water-tube boiler, the boiler tubes are built inside the furnace. The hot combustion gases inside the furnace heat the outside surface of the tubes. When water flows through the tubes, heat is transferred though the tubes and into the water moving through them. The combustion gases of the furnace are sent from the furnace to the filtering system and out the stack.

Water run through the deaerator of a boiler is frequently stored in tanks mounted on top of the boiler structure. The water used in boilers must be kept extremely clean. It is usually chemically treated to prevent corrosion and blockage in the small boiler tubes. Chemicals and the chemical-injection equipment are normally located inside a water treatment area built close to the boiler. Over time, and sometimes because of poor maintenance, the tubes inside a boiler will fail. Maintenance personnel may have to climb inside a boiler to check or repair tubes that are leaking or broken. Such work can obviously be performed only after the furnace and tubes have cooled. Work inside boilers is confined space work and may be dangerous. All safety precautions must be followed when working in boilers.

Warning:

WARNING!

Only personnel trained and certified for confined space work should enter confined spaces. In most, if not all cases, tower work is permit-required confined space work. All safety procedures must be followed at all times.

The water passing through the boilers may be sent to tubes located in other vessels and returned to the boiler system for reheating. The heated water passing through the tubes of the user vessels transfers the heat into the vessel or into a product inside that vessel.

Boilers are rated by boiler horsepower (hp). Fire-tube boilers are usually used in low-pressure applications (around 100 boiler hp). Water-tube boilers are used at higher-pressure applications because they are structurally stronger.
