6.2.3 Torquing Conventional Nuts

Conventional nuts and bolts may or may not have washers placed between the nut and the surface of the components being connected. The nuts should be threaded down their bolts far enough to take up any slack before the torquing tool is installed. Preloading the bolts and nuts ensures that the pieces are properly mated before the torquing process begins. If the bolts are loose on the surface of the opposite flange member, use a wrench strong enough to secure the bolt head during the torquing activities. Also make sure that the wrench is securely installed. The hydraulic hoses should have already been connected between the hydraulic system and the tools.

Before any kind of lubricants are applied to the bolt threads or the nuts, check assembly specifications for the members being assembled.

Perform the following steps to torque conventional nuts:

  1. Obtain a socket to fit the nut being tightened. If needed, also make sure a wrench is properly installed and secured on the bolt head at the opposite side of the member being attached.

  2. Insert the square drive attachment into the socket and place the socket onto the nut.

  3. Set the pressure of the hydraulic system to the first desired torquing level and lock the pump setting.

  4. Apply pressure to the tool in two or three quick shots and cycle the tool to ensure that it is operating properly.

  5. Position the torquing tool into the torquing position and lower it onto the square drive attachment.

Figure 24 shows square drive attachment, its retainer cover, and ways it can be installed in the torquing tool.

  1. Ensure that the tool is properly coupled to the square drive attachment.

  2. Ensure that the tool is positioned with its reaction pad securely mounted against a firm object.

  3. Ensure that all hoses are free of any obstacles.

  4. Apply pressure to the tool and ensure that the reaction point is stable.

    Note:

    NOTE

    Different tools and hydraulic system controls have different pressure controls, so no picture of the control will be shown at this point. When pressure is applied to the tool, its rear end will push down against the reaction pad.

  5. Continue applying pressure until a click is heard.

  6. Repeat the cycles until the first specified level of torque is applied to the nut and bolt.

  7. Release pressure to the tool and remove the tool from the square drive attachment.

  8. Remove the socket and drive attachment from the nut and verify that neither is damaged.

  9. Move the socket and drive attachment to the next nut and repeat the process.

  10. Continue torquing all the nuts until they all have been torqued to the initial point.

  11. Reset the hydraulic system for the next level of torque, and then repeat the process on all the bolts.

    Note:

    NOTE

    When each nut is torqued to the final level, the tool will stall. To ensure that it has applied all the pressure it will apply, cycle the tool one more time before removing it for the final time.

  12. Remove all the tools and associated hardware and inspect them for damage.

  13. Store tools and associated hardware as directed.
