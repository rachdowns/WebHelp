6.2.2 Torquing and Tensioning Safety

Torquing and tensioning devices can be dangerous. They may also be in use in a potentially dangerous environment. For those reasons, personnel using these tools must strictly follow all vendor manual safety guidelines in addition to local safety guidelines. Here are a few safety suggestions:

  • Because these tools are often used at the equipment in the field, portable hydraulic systems are normally required to support them. If there are potentially explosive vapors or dust in the work environment, pneumatic pumps may be required to drive the hydraulic systems.

  • To prevent electrical shock, ensure that all equipment is properly grounded.

  • Ensure that all hydraulic lines and connectors are in good condition and properly connected.

  • Do not kink or pinch hoses.

  • Inspect each tool and make sure that it is in good operating condition with all guards in place.

  • Verify that each tool has been calibrated within the past year.

  • Ensure that the pump and tool(s) are operated only by a designated operator.

  • Tools are designed to be hands free, but if a tool must be secured by hand, make sure that the remote operator knows how to properly control the pump.

  • Call “All Clear” prior to starting the pump.

  • Before placing a tool onto a nut, test it to ensure that it is functioning properly.

  • Ensure that the right socket is selected for each nut being tightened.

  • When positioning a torquing and tensioning tool for torquing, make sure that the tool is properly positioned for tightening (remember that they must be turned over when loosening nuts) and that the hydraulic hoses to it are not in a position to be pinched.

  • Attach the tool and socket to the nut being tightened.

  • Pressurize the hydraulic system for just a moment to see how the tool reacts. If it rides up or creeps, stop and readjust the reaction arm to a more secure location. If everything appears to be correct, begin the process.

Before starting any torquing or tensioning job, review the engineering specifications for the process equipment on which the work will be performed. Make sure of the following:

  • That all torque and tensioning levels are clearly understood.

  • That all work area hazards are understood and appropriate precautions are taken.

  • That the hydraulic pumping system being used has sufficient power to support the tools performing the work.

  • That all bolts and nuts are new or else approved by the engineer responsible.

Warning:

WARNING!

Always follow all safety procedures and practices when working with hydraulic torquing and tensioning equipment, to prevent possible injury or death. The pressures involved are capable of explosive damage to equipment and danger to personnel.
