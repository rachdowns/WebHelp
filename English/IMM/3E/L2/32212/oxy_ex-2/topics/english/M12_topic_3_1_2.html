2.1.2 Destructive Examinations

Destructive exams are generally more expensive than NDE, so they are used when simpler forms of NDE cannot detect the flaw. For some components, a complete examination can only be done by removing and analyzing test samples. Destructive testing is usually done to determine the method of failure when it is not easily identified.

Destructive testing is done for two types of specimens: tube samples and boat samples.

Tubes are the component most routinely tested using destructive testing. A relatively large number of samples may be removed for visual inspections and a small number of these are selected for complete laboratory analysis.

A tube analysis may include any or all of the following tests, depending on ease of figuring out a cause of failure:

  • As-received photo documentation

  • Complete visual inspection

  • Dimensional evaluation of a section removed from the sample

  • Material verification

  • Optical metallography

  • Material hardness measurement

For waterwall tubes, the analysis includes a measurement of the internal deposit loading and elemental makeup of the deposit. For steam-cooled superheater and reheater tubes, the thickness of the high-temperature oxide layer is also measured. These tests will determine the relative overall health of the components and may indicate whether further testing or replacement is required.

Specialized tests for failure analysis are performed when required, to provide more in-depth information. When a failure occurs in which the root cause is not readily known from standard tests, fractography is performed. Fractography involves examination of the fracture surface using a scanning electron microscope.

Boat samples are wedge-shaped slivers removed from larger components such as headers, piping, and drums. The shape of the cut allows the material to be replaced by welding. Because the repairs usually require postweld heat treating, the use of boat samples is expensive. In most instances, another form of NDE will reveal the cause of failure, and boat samples will not be required.
