Section Two

2.0.0 IDENTIFYING TUBE PROBLEMS

Warning:

WARNING!

No job is ever so important that safety can be ignored. The number one priority in tube work is safety.

Planning boiler work to ensure that no unplanned shutdowns occur due to failure is known as condition assessment. This has been performed for years in the boiler industry. Tube and header failures cause the majority of boiler downtime. Since boiler startups and shutdowns are more frequent now, the need for routine condition assessment has become more important in the effort to prevent future problems before they cause unplanned shutdowns.

Condition assessment determines:

  • How much time is left before a component fails

  • Which components must be worked on immediately

Note:

NOTE

Verify that all materials are of the proper type prior to starting work.

Figure 1 shows the flow path of the three phases of boiler damage (condition) assessment. In Phase I of the assessment, operating records are reviewed to establish a baseline. In Phase II, nondestructive examinations, stress testing, verification of dimensions, and operating conditions are performed. When required, Phase III includes elemental analysis, operational testing and evaluation, and material properties measurement.

Warning:

WARNING!

When performing inspections for tube work, always wear appropriate personal protective equipment to prevent injury or death.
