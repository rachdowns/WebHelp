3.3.0 Tube Placement (Sticking)

A representative number of unrepaired drum holes should be measured in two directions, 90 degrees apart, to establish a mean drum hole diameter. This number is used in determining the increase in tube inside diameter (ID) required during tube end expansion. Approximately 10 percent of unrepaired drum holes should be selected at random for this measurement. Any drum holes with a diameter measuring 0.041 inch greater than the tube outside diameter (OD) should not be included when establishing the mean. If a significant number of sampled holes are oversized, then individual readings should be taken for each hole and those values used to calculate rolling requirements rather than using an average value. All repaired drum holes should be measured, as there is a greater likelihood that those holes are enlarged.

The OD of a representative sample of tube ends should be measured to establish the mean OD. Similarly, the mean ID should be established. These numbers (and the wall thickness calculated by difference) will be used in the calculation for wall reduction. A reasonable sample size for tube ends is 5 percent.

Tube ends and drum holes should be inspected and cleaned prior to placing the tubes into the holes. The steam drum end of the tube should be cleaned over the full length of the swage. If there is no swage, the steam drum end should be cleaned over a length equal to the thickness of the steam drum, plus the thickness of the mud drum, plus 3 inches. This prevents dragging of foreign material into the tube hole when inserting the tube. The mud drum end should be cleaned over a length equal to the thickness of the mud drum plus 3 inches.

  • The surfaces should appear metal clean after buffing when examined without magnification under normal lighting conditions.

  • The surfaces should be free of particulate contaminants such as sand, metal chips, welding slag, etc.

  • The surface should be free of organic films and contaminants such as oils, paint, and preservatives as determined by a visual examination, use of a solvent-dampened white-cloth, or an equivalent alternate method.

  • When a visual inspection is not possible and the surfaces are accessible, a dry white-cloth wipe, followed by a solvent-dampened white-cloth wipe, may be used to evaluate the surface cleanliness. If either cloth exhibits indications of contamination, the surface should be re-cleaned.

  • The ID of the tube section to be rolled should be free of particulate contaminants as was just mentioned. This will help prevent slipping of the rolls during expansion, and it will also minimize expander wear.

Steam drum tube ends are inserted first, and then the mud drum end. Per ASME code, the tube ends shall project through the tube sheet or headers not less than 1⁄4 inch and not more than 3⁄4 inch before flaring. Where tubes enter at an angle, the maximum limit of 3⁄4 inch shall apply only at the point of least projection. The tubes shall be expanded, and flared to an outside diameter of at least 1⁄8 inch greater than the diameter of the drum hole.

Tube alignment should be established using hardwood spacer blocks cut to the exact side space clearance dimensions. The spacer blocks are inserted between the rows of tubes when establishing stock and soft rolling. Back spacing is normally set and monitored visually.

Tubes should be placed and soft rolled within twenty-four hours of cleaning and inspection. Normally the number of rows of tubes inserted before soft rolling is limited to three or four rows to allow accessibility for setting stock and removal of spacer blocks.
