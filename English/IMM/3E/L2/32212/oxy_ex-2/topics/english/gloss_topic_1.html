Glossary

Authorized inspector (AI)

: Authorized inspectors are designated by the insurance company and have received special training to perform boiler inspections.

Boat samples

: One of two types of samples used for destructive testing. A wedge-shape slice is removed from larger equipment, such as headers, piping, and drums. This method of sample removal makes the removal section easier to repair.

Creep life

: When a tube is put into service, the metal contacting the steam begins to form oxide scale known as magnetite (Fe3O4). As the tube is further aged, the layer grows at a rate dependent on the tube environment and tube temperature.

Erosion-corrosion

: Wall thinning that is flow-induced and occurs on the fluid side of the piping system.

Fatigue damage

: Damage to a piping system caused by repeated cyclic loading, which is the result of vibration, thermal expansion, and contraction.

Ligament cracks

: Cracks that have developed between tube holes.

Necking

: A reduction in overall volume due to stretching.

Peanut

: A filler for a welded membrane.

Pendant

: The tubing supply and return U-bend part hanging down on a superheater.

Steam-cooled tubing

: Tubing found in the superheater (high pressure) and reheat superheater. Both components have tubes subjected to the effects of metal creep.

Tube samples

: One of two types of samples used for destructive testing. They are generally removed from water-cooled and steam-cooled circuits.

Tube washing

: Using a torch with a brazing or welding tip, the tube end is heated until a melting process begins. The oxygen-enriched atmosphere causes the metal to be blown away from the site with minimal tube sheet damage.

Water-cooled tubing

: Includes tubing in the economizer, boiler bank, and furnace. The convection pass sidewall and screen tubes may also be water-cooled. These tubes operate at or below saturation temperature and are not subject to significant creep.
