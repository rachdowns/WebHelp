3.2.0 Tube Sheet and Tubing End Hole Preparation

Drum holes should be given a preliminary visual inspection after the stub ends are removed and prior to cleaning for the earliest detection of damage. If damage is noted, appropriate corrective action should be taken immediately and repairs scheduled.

Drum holes should then be cleaned of debris, verified free from defects, and re-inspected. Cleaning should be accomplished using an emery flapper wheel in a circular motion. Grooves and counter bore are-as should be free of debris, but they do not need to be polished to bright metal. In certain instances, the use of an abrasive stone may be required to remove a buildup of hard scale from the surface of the tube seat, especially if the tube was not originally expanded for the entire depth of the tube seat. If the serrations have been damaged during the tube removal process, the tube hole must have a weld buildup procedure performed, then the hole must be redrilled to the correct size and a special tool must be used to reinstall the serrations.

Ligament cracks in drums or headers are cracks that have developed between tube holes. These cracks may be repairable, but the repairs should only be undertaken by the boiler contractor with the full knowledge and/or participation of the insurance company and the owner.

The edges of the drum hole adjacent to the seat area should be free of burrs or upset metal. Any sharp-bottomed nicks located on the water-side edge of the tube seat area caused by tools such as chisels should be rounded out. Weld repair is not necessary if the depth of the nick is within the allowable limits.

Gouges in the tube seat area should be repaired if the depth of the gouge exceeds the depth of the machined grooves, or if the length of the gouge on the seating surface extends more than half the distance between the edges of any seating surface.

Weld repairs should be performed in accordance with the National Board Inspection Code (NBIC) using ASME-qualified welding procedures (WPS) and welders. ASME Section IX must be used for any welded repairs to the tube seats in the boiler drums. The type of welding procedures used should be developed and agreed upon prior to starting the job. Special attention should be placed on the preheat requirements, especially the method of preheat and the procedure for verifying that the preheat is maintained through-out the weld procedure. The tube seat should be re-cleaned and re-inspected after repair is complete.
