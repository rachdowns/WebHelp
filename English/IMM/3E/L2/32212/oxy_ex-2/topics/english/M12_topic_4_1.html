3.1.0 Tubing Extraction

After evaluating which repairs are necessary and which components have to be removed, the removal process begins.

CAUTION:

CAUTION

Tube extraction must be done carefully to prevent damage to the drum shell.

Use the following general procedure to remove tubing from drums, headers, and tube plates:

  1. Downcomers, header riser tubes, and any generating bank tubes that will not be replaced should be covered or plugged before any work is performed inside the drums. This prevents foreign material from being placed inside or falling into a tube. The owner should verify that the covers are in place and secured.

  2. Prior to tube removal, the owner should ensure that the tubes to be removed are correctly identified on both the inside and outside of each drum. This should be reviewed with the contractor at the beginning of the job and at the start of each shift with the individual supervisor.

  3. As an option, mole jetting can be used to clean the tubes. The mole jet is self-propelled through the tube by the action of the high-pressure water. The mole jet head is attached to a stinger (11⁄2 pipe-diameters long to prevent reversal in the tube) and the stinger is attached to a hose, all of which is inserted into the tube and held by a clamp bolted on one end of the tube.

    Warning:

    WARNING!

    Hydroblasting operations are dangerous and should be done only by trained personnel wearing the proper safety equipment. For more information on hydroblasting, see the Contren® Learning Series Hydroblasting curriculum.

  4. To support either the mud or steam drum during the retubing process, a number of generating bank tubes must remain in place until a sufficient number of new tubes have been installed. Be-fore starting tube removal, these support tubes should be identified and clearly marked. Support tubes can be removed once a predefined number of new tubes have been installed.

  5. The indicated tubes should be carefully cut and removed from the boiler. Any remaining tubes should be inspected for signs of damage while they are readily accessible.

There are several methods for removal of stub ends from drums. These methods should be discussed with the contractor during the qualification process. Drilling and use of an oxyacetylene torch with a brazing tip are two of these methods. A hydraulic tube puller (which doesn’t work for serrated tube sheets) is used for tubes that are in a tube sheet.

Note:

NOTE

The use of an oxyacetylene torch with a brazing tip is referred to as torch cutting through the remainder of this module.

Tube washing is significantly faster than the other two procedures, and is by far the most common procedure in use. It is also the most operator dependent. Only qualified personnel should participate in these tasks because of the potential for damage to the tube seats. The other two methods have been tried in an effort to reduce the potential for tube seat damage. The key to a successful project is crew pre-qualification, and early, frequent inspection once the job has begun.

Boilers with tubes that have been both rolled and seal welded will require removal of the seal weld prior to removal of the stub ends. Special milling tools are available for removal of the seal welds. The milling tools will also remove a small amount of drum metal on the front and back side of the tube seat because of the curvature of the drum plate. This reduction in drum thickness should be reviewed with the original equipment manufacturer (OEM) and approved by the authorized inspector (AI) before using this procedure. The tube seats should be tested after removal of the tube to check for any cracks associated with the seal weld. Seal welding of the replacement tubes should be performed using the OEM’s welding procedure or equivalent. Special attention should be given to the preheat requirements, especially the method of preheat and the procedure for verifying that the preheat is maintained throughout the weld procedure. All seal welds should have completed NDE to check for cracking.

Tube washing is the quickest and most common method of tube stub end removal. In tube washing, the tube end is heated with a torch that has a brazing or welding tip until it begins to melt. There are two basic methods of torch-cutting removal of stub ends. One method is to score (melt), collapse, and drive the stub end from the outside into the drum. The other method is to score and drive the stub end from the drum using a mandrel and hammer.

Using the score, collapse, and drive method, a torch is used to make longitudinal melts through the length of the stub end in two or three areas. The stub end is then collapsed and driven out with a punch and hammer.

Using the score and drive method, a torch is used to melt through the stub end at two locations 180 degrees apart. A mandrel and hammer are used to drive the stub end from the drum. The bell (flared end of the tube inside the drum) must be removed from the stub ends in the steam drum.

Note:

NOTE

The score and drive procedure works well on ungrooved tube holes. If the tube sheet is serrated, there is a greater chance of damaging the drum hole when using the score and drive procedure. The score, collapse, and drive method should be used for serrated tube sheets.

CAUTION:

CAUTION

Air-arc gouging and oxyacetylene cutting torches with oxygen levers should not be used for stub removal because there is an increased potential for damage to tube seats.

The hydraulic tube puller method of tube removal is the least dependent on operator experience and expertise. It does not require cutting and collapsing of the stub end, and eliminates the potential for tube seat damage associated with those steps. However, hydraulic pulling is not in wide use by contractors for drum tube work because the equipment is expensive, stub end removal is slower, and when used on serrated tube sheets there is a greater potential to damage drum holes during stub end removal. The hydraulic tube puller method is used for tubes that are set into tube sheets or are thin walled and/or loosely rolled. This method works extremely well for these types of tubes.

During the tube removal process, the possibility exists that an adjacent tube may be nicked or a tube sheet may be damaged. If any damage is caused during the tube removal process, inform your supervisor.
