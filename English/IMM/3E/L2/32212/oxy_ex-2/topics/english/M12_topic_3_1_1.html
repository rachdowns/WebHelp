2.1.1 Nondestructive Examinations

Most nondestructive examination (NDE) methods have been in use for many years. The NDE methods used to evaluate boiler plants include:

  • Visual inspection – Using your knowledge of the equipment design and wear characteristics, determine where work or repair is required. This type of inspection can detect surface flaws.

  • Magnetic/wet fluorescent particle (MT/WFMT) – This type of NDE uses a magnetic field driven through the area of inspection. The magnetic field passing through the inspection area will cause the dry or wet magnetic particles to line up. If a defect is present, it will cause a disturbance in the layout of the particles that can be viewed either with the naked eye for dry magnetic tests, or with an ultraviolet light for wet fluorescent particles. This type of test can find flaws that are on or close to the surface.

  • Liquid penetrant (PT) – Testing using this method is useful if the flaw extends to the surface of the material. Capillary action draws the penetrant into the flaw. When the penetrant is dry, a developer is sprayed onto the area. Once the penetrant and the developer are in contact with each other, the flaw be-comes obvious.

  • Ultrasonic wave (UT)/ultrasonic shear wave – Using sound which cannot be heard by humans, a wave is sent into the material. Defects will reflect the sound back. This testing requires specialized equipment and training to ensure the operator can interpret the display correctly to identify flaws. The ultrasonic shear wave uses a shear wave at an angle between 45 and 60 degrees for testing.

  • Eddy current – This test is somewhat like the MT. A current is induced by an alternating magnetic field through the test area, and the resulting eddy currents are analyzed to detect the flaw.

  • Radiography (RT) – X-rays or gamma rays are passed through the test area to expose film that is placed on the opposite side of the radiation source. If a void is present in a weld, more rays will pass through the void and expose the film. This will show up as a dark spot on the film. Using special equipment and training, RT can detect flaws in very thick materials. The major disadvantage of RT is that the radioactive rays necessary for this type of testing can be harmful to humans. This type of testing is used extensively when the boiler is first erected to verify pipe and tubing welds.

  • Nuclear fluorescence – Like RT, nuclear fluorescence uses a radioactive source. Certain materials (such as chromium), when exposed to radioactivity, will give off (emit) X-rays. The test equipment has a small radioactive source in it and exposes the material. Many of the elements in the pipe surface will emit X-rays when exposed. The rest of the test equipment takes those X-rays and analyzes them for content. This type of testing is used to verify the composition of materials.

  • Electromagnetic acoustics – This type of testing uses two magnetic sources that interact to produce a shock pulse that is used for testing. The shock pulse generated can then be used like the UT pulse. This type of testing only works on materials that can conduct electricity.

  • Metallographic replication – By using a special tape, a replica of the grain structure of metal can be gathered and viewed using a microscope. First, the area is roughed up using a grinder and flapper wheel. Next, the test area is polished using fine sandpaper and diamond paste while an acid solution is applied. The acid solution will attack the edges of the graining in the metal. Once the area is prepared, the tape is applied to the area. Once the tape is mounted onto a slide, it can be analyzed through a micro-scope.

In this module we will only discuss the visual inspection method of NDE. Many of the other tests re-quire extensive special training to become qualified to operate the test equipment.

If the component is damaged due to mechanical wear, chemical, or thermal stress, a good visual examination will allow you to spot most types of damage. When you perform visual inspections, you should have good lighting, some type of magnification for a closer inspection, and a mirror to ensure you are able to look at all sides of a component. When performing visual inspections, any flaw, regardless of size, should be brought to your supervisor’s attention. Always visually inspect as much as you can to ensure the job is done right the first time.
