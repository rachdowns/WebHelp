5.4.0 Individual Thermostatic Valves

All steam heating systems need some means of controlling the temperature in the conditioned space. In some systems, it is done by controlling the temperature input to the entire system. In other systems, it may be done by controlling the temperature of individual spaces or zones. Some systems use a combination of both. Individual thermostatic valves can be installed at the input to room terminals as a means of zone temperature control. There are many types of thermostatic control valves. These valves can be activated either by an electric or pneumatic signal applied from a centralized system or zone control. Some individual thermostatic controls have a built-in mechanism and related sensing element that needs no external power source. This type of control is a variable volume flow (modulating) control.
