Glossary

Attrition

: Motion under applied pressure that will cause particle breakup due to friction.

Black liquor

: A byproduct of the papermaking process. It consists of a mixture of limestone, saltcake, water, and lignin.

Carryover

: State of having solids present in the steam and condensate lines. This can result in production losses due to blockages. A health hazard can exist if the affected lines are used for cleaning, sterilization, and the processing of a product.

Classifier

: A device that is used to determine the final size of the coal particles from a pulverizer.

Clinkering

: The production of large amounts of ash slag.

Condensate

: Steam which has been condensed for return to the boiler.

Condensate polishing

: In-line treatment used to remove impurities from condensate returns to prevent introduction into the boiler.

Dew point

: The temperature at which air becomes saturated and water droplets in the air will condense onto cooler surfaces.

Eductor

: A device that uses a venturi effect to create an area of lower pressure. The venturi effect comes from running a liquid or gas stream through an orifice. This creates an area of lower pressure on the downstream side of the orifice. If a pipe is then attached from another system to the downstream side, a vacuum is available to the connected system.

Feedwater

: The total flow of water supplied to the boiler. It is the sum of all condensate sources plus makeup water sources.

Fines

: Finely crushed or powdered material, or very small particles in a mixture of various sizes.

Ion

: An atom which has an abundance or deficiency of electrons in its atomic structure. This results in the atom having an overall positive (loss of electrons) or negative (excess electrons) charge.

Matting

: The formation of layers of ash slag on heat transfer surfaces.

Quench

: To remove heat from a material quickly by thrusting into water or other liquid.

Reagent

: A substance that when added to a chemical process, either takes part in or causes a chemical reaction to take place.

Resin

: A substance that is made up of ions (positive and negative), used to remove unwanted ions from water.

Retort

: The central trough in an underfeed stoker.

Sintering

: To form a mass of uniform composition from many substances due to high temperature with-out melting.

Slake

: To cause a material (lime) to crumble by the addition of water.

Translation speed

: The rate at which a soot blower lance is extended into a boiler.

Tuyere

: The pipe, nozzle, or other opening where air is forced into the furnace to facilitate combustion.

Volatile chemicals

: Chemicals that are easily vaporized. The benefit of volatile chemicals is that the chemicals are vaporized with the steam and are recondensed in the condensate system that minimizes chemical addition and eases chemistry control.
